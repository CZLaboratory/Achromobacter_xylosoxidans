Development of predictive understanding of nutrient channeling and metabolism across different growth stages is critical to combat infectious diseases. Among those antibiotic-resistant bacteria, Achromobacter xylosoxidans (Ax), an emerging opportunistic pathogen, is present across hosts and environments worldwide, overcoming natural communities. 
Here, we developed a model-driven pangenome analysis of all available Ax genomics data, adding 22% more new genomes and revealing an uncharacterized non-canonical nitrification pathway. Genome-scale metabolic model simulations provided quantitative insights into the shift to an alternative energy-generating strategy based on specific carbon utilization. Experimental validation showed that nitrification was intermittent and associated with the late exponential phase. 
An expanded flux-to-AI approach revealed that the phenotypic nutrient preferences, such as dipeptide Gly-Met, acetoacetate, and cysteine, drive a host-dependent metabolism. These nutrient trade-offs also serve as inflection points in non-canonical nitrification, providing a rational understanding of the metabolic plasticity of Ax that can be extended to other pathogens.
